What is Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU)?

An Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) is a smaller, fully independent secondary dwelling unit that is built on an already existing property in your area.

Simply put, it’s a second complete house or apartment on the same lot, usually featuring its own kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, and separate entrance for privacy.

Many people know it as a “secondary housing unit” or “in-law suite,” but its legal and construction value is far more complex and significant than those terms suggest.

ADUs are a flexible and intelligent response to the lack of affordable housing and the growing need for comfortable and private family accommodation in the state.

The goal is to create a space that is permanently habitable, whether for an extended family member or to generate a crucial stream of rental income for you.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

You likely have questions about building an Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) in the state, which is perfectly normal for a project of this size and long-term importance.

Our Construction company in Massachusetts compiled the 7 most frequent questions we receive from homeowners like you to clarify the process and common concerns right away.

Can I rent out my ADU for income?

Yes, in most Massachusetts cities, current laws allow you to rent out your Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) to non-family members, which is a key benefit.

Renting the unit is the most common way our clients generate passive income to offset high mortgage costs or to secure extra retirement funds.

What size can my ADU be?

The size restrictions vary greatly by municipality, but generally, the unit size cannot exceed the main home’s living area or a maximum square footage set by the city.

Our team will verify the specific local zoning rules to ensure your Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) is fully compliant and maximizes the allowable space for you.

How long does the entire process take?

From initial design to final completion, the entire process usually takes between 9 to 18 months, with permit approval being the most variable and often time-consuming step.

Our Construction company in Massachusetts works efficiently to manage the timeline and keeps you updated on milestones, especially during the crucial permit waiting periods.

Do I need an extra parking spot for the ADU?

In many areas of Massachusetts, especially in cities like Boston and surrounding towns, new regulations often waive or relax the requirement for an additional parking spot for an ADU.

We will confirm this detail based on your specific address, ensuring all municipal requirements are met for your Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU).

Will an ADU increase my property taxes?

Yes, because the ADU increases the assessed value of your property, your property taxes will increase after the unit is completed and officially assessed.

However, the significant income generated by the Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) typically outweighs the increase in property tax, making it a net financial gain.

What about utility connections for the ADU?

The ADU must have its own utility connections or be connected to the main house’s systems, depending on local code and your specific design plan.

Our Construction company in Massachusetts handles all the necessary plumbing and electrical work to ensure the Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) is fully functional and safe.

Is my property eligible for an ADU?

Eligibility depends on local zoning laws, lot size, and setbacks, but most single-family zoned lots in Massachusetts are now eligible under state-level initiatives.
